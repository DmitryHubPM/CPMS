Prototype User Guide

Clinical Budgeting and Project Management System

A drill-down clinical study planning prototype where a budget line becomes an expandable work unit with composition, schedule, location, evidence, payment logic, cost, resources, documents, and earned value tracking.

Illustration only. The demo uses public protocol references and simplified assumptions. Budget numbers, contract text, monitoring language, PM plan language, SOP placeholders, and schedule outputs are not correct for contracting, regulatory use, quality system use, or operational decision-making.

1. Purpose Of The System

The system demonstrates a planning approach for clinical research where the budget is not a flat spreadsheet. Each budget line is a unit of work that can be planned at a high level first, then progressively detailed as information becomes available.

What It Starts With

  • Protocol-derived units and assumptions
  • Study phases and milestones
  • Global unit counts when countries and sites are not known
  • Role-based labor assumptions
  • Pass-through and investigator grant assumptions

What It Becomes

  • Country, site, and visit-level schedules
  • Resource and FTE demand
  • Timesheet plan and actual hours
  • Evidence and payment status tracking
  • Change order, OOS, and contract support

2. Budgeting Process

  1. Extract units from the protocol. Identify visits, start-up activities, monitoring work, safety work, data work, PM work, reporting work, and close-out or follow-up activities.
  2. Create budget units. Each unit has a phase, milestone, count, location assumption, dates, and cost model.
  3. Define composition. Add departments, roles, functions, planned hours per unit, hourly rates, and optional employee assignment.
  4. Split detail progressively. Start with Global. Later split to region, country, site, and exact planned dates.
  5. Track completion and evidence. A completed unit becomes validated only when required evidence is attached and the document status is Final or Filed.
  6. Track payment logic. Units may be paid based on completion, document filing, sponsor approval, or manual approval.
  7. Recalculate reports. Cost, resource schedule, cash flow, timesheets, evidence status, and EVA update from the same data.
  8. Control commercial status. Keep work in Contract Unit, approved Change Order, or OOS category.

6. Unit Drill-Down

After saving a budget row, open it as a unit card. The card contains the operational detail.

Detail Builder

Shows maturity from Global to Region to Country to Site, from Department to Role to Employee, and from planned dates to actual completed units.

Unit Composition

Edit role rows, hours, rates, function, department, employee, and assumption type.

Duration & Schedule

Generate dates between start and end dates, then override exact planned dates when known.

Completion & Evidence

Record actual completion, actual hours, completed by, evidence type, evidence status, and proof link placeholder.

Payment Logic

Track investigator payment, sponsor reimbursement, and payment triggers.

Cost Summary

Calculate planned cost, actual cost, variance, validated cost, not validated cost, and open exposure.

8. Contract, Change Order, And OOS Logic

Each budget row has a commercial source:

  • Contract Unit Baseline scope included in the original contract or work order.
  • CO1, CO2, CO3 Approved or planned change order scope.
  • OOS1, OOS2, OOS3 Out-of-scope work not yet converted to a change order.

This allows the same operational budget to support contract control, budget control, and project reporting.

9. Document Constructor

The Contracts & Docs tab is a prototype document constructor. It uses the live budget grid to summarize commercial scope and creates document backbones.

  • Study Work Order / Clinical Services Agreement draft sections
  • Commercial summary from budget grid
  • Change Order / OOS register
  • Project Management Plan draft sections
  • Monitoring Plan draft sections
  • SOP and TMF placeholder list

10. Recommended Demo Workflow

  1. Start on the Plan tab and review the Budget Story and KPIs.
  2. Open the Budget Grid and change one row from Contract Unit to OOS1, then Save.
  3. Use Country split to show how global units become countries, sites, and TBD remainder.
  4. Open a unit card and show the Detail Builder maturity ladder.
  5. Move to Duration & Schedule and edit a planned date.
  6. Move to Completion & Evidence and mark an item completed, then change evidence from Draft to Filed.
  7. Show that Budget, Resources, Cash Flow, Evidence, EVA, and Contracts & Docs update from the same underlying model.

11. Current Prototype Limits

  • No backend, database, login, audit trail, validation package, role security, or electronic signature.
  • No real document storage. Document links are placeholders.
  • No real protocol parsing pipeline. The current demo uses simplified manual assumptions.
  • No real legal contract generation. Contract language is sample structure only.
  • No regulatory-ready SOPs. SOP names and sections are placeholders for product concept discussion.
  • No persistent save after browser refresh unless the code is extended with local storage or backend storage.
